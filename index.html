FAQ

Common questions from
public works teams

The questions infrastructure directors ask before deployment.

How does CivisIntel connect to existing sensor infrastructure?
CivisIntel integrates with all major IoT sensor protocols including MQTT, OPC-UA, REST, and proprietary formats from manufacturers like National Instruments, HBK, and Micro-Epsilon. For new deployments, we support a CivisIntel-certified sensor kit that can be installed by any licensed electrician without structural modification.
What does 98.7% accuracy actually mean in practice?
It means that in backtesting against 60+ years of historical structural failure events, CivisIntel's models correctly predicted 98.7% of failures within the forecast window — with a false positive rate below 4%. Every prediction includes a confidence interval and the specific sensor signatures driving it, so your engineers can validate the reasoning before acting.
Is the platform FedRAMP authorized?
CivisIntel is currently FedRAMP Moderate-authorized for federal agency use and is in the process of achieving FedRAMP High authorization. All data is processed and stored within US-based infrastructure, with full FISMA compliance and optional air-gapped deployment for sensitive national infrastructure.
How long does deployment take?
For agencies with existing sensor networks, CivisIntel can be live in 48–72 hours. Full deployments including sensor installation, baseline calibration, and staff training typically complete in 4–6 weeks. We assign a dedicated deployment engineer to every government client at no additional cost.
Can we procure CivisIntel through GSA Schedule?
Yes. CivisIntel is listed on GSA Schedule 70 (IT Services) and eligible for procurement through SAM.gov cooperative purchasing. Our team can provide all documentation required for sole-source justification for municipalities and state agencies.
